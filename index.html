Identity != Permissions

Authorization is hard - and authorization is often conflated with authentication and identity. Many vendors try to sell you "Identity and Access Management" as a single product. We think that this is wrong, and those two concerns must be clearly separared.

This most often lead to problematic situations down the line - we wrote a popular blog post that expressed our opinions by the time.

Identity + Permissions == Authorization

We think identity must be input to authorization system to produce the actual authorization policies. While there are many ways to model authorization, we found that the concept of roles and permissions are the most prevalent. implementing authorization in web applications and APIs - Dominick Baier & Brock Allen from NDC Conferences on Vimeo.

PolicyServer

This all lead to prototyping "PolicyServer" - an implementation of the above pattern. We decided to split PolicyServer into two parts - a free OSS version that has all the necessary patterns and libraries to achieve the separatio of authentication and authorization - and a commercial version which deals with more advanced scenarios around distribution and management.
